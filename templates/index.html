Why you should recycle your clothes?

When planning for your next big night out, a job interview, or holiday, you might think to buy a new outfit. But have you considered what the impact of fast fashion might be? UK households have an average of £4,000 worth of clothes - but 30% of those haven’t been worn in the last year! We can all save money and help the environment by making simple changes to the way we buy, use and get rid of our clothing.
When clothing isn’t recycled, it means more and more has to be produced and that means more and more pollution for all of us. Pesticides used in the production of clothing enter our ground water and streams affecting bees, animals and the farm workers who have to work with them. Dyes, most of which contain heavy metals, are known to be harmful to public health. Synthetic polyesters and nylon are made from petrochemicals, a byproduct of oil refining which increases our reliance on oil and endangers aquatic life as seen in Blue Planet!

  • Reduces greenhouse gases
  • Saves landfill space
  • Conserves enery
  • Benefits people
  • Helps people in need
